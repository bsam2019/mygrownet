# MyGrowNet Board Members and Shareholders Policy

**Company Name**: Rockshield Investments Limited (trading as MyGrowNet)  
**Document Type**: Corporate Governance Policy  
**Effective Date**: November 3, 2025  
**Version**: 1.0  
**Status**: Official Policy Document

## 1. Introduction and Purpose

### 1.1 Purpose

This policy document establishes the governance framework for Rockshield Investments Limited (hereinafter "the Company" or "MyGrowNet"), defining the roles, rights, responsibilities, and obligations of Board Members and Shareholders in accordance with Zambian law and best corporate governance practices.

### 1.2 Scope

This policy applies to:
- All members of the Board of Directors
- All shareholders of the Company
- Executive management team
- Any person or entity with governance or ownership interest in the Company

### 1.3 Legal Framework

This policy is established under:
- The Companies Act of Zambia (Chapter 388 of the Laws of Zambia)
- The Company's Articles of Association
- The Company's Memorandum of Association
- Relevant regulations from the Patents and Companies Registration Agency (PACRA)
- Bank of Zambia regulations (where applicable)
- Securities and Exchange Commission guidelines (where applicable)

### 1.4 Company Structure

MyGrowNet operates as a **Private Limited Company** with a hybrid business model:

**Corporate Structure:**
- Registered private limited company under Zambian law
- Shareholders own equity in the Company
- Board of Directors provides governance oversight
- Executive management handles daily operations

**Member-Based Operations:**
- Platform members subscribe for products and services
- Members earn through referrals, commissions, and profit-sharing
- Members may participate in Venture Builder co-investment opportunities
- Members do NOT own equity in MyGrowNet (unless they are also shareholders)

**Critical Distinction:**
- **Shareholders** = Equity owners of Rockshield Investments Limited
- **Members** = Subscribers to MyGrowNet platform services
- These are separate and distinct roles (though individuals may hold both)

## 2. Definitions

For the purposes of this policy, the following definitions apply:

**Board Member / Director**: An individual appointed to serve on the Board of Directors of the Company, responsible for governance oversight and strategic direction.

**Shareholder**: An individual or entity that owns shares in Rockshield Investments Limited, holding equity ownership in the Company.

**Member**: An individual who subscribes to MyGrowNet platform services, products, and programs. Members are customers/subscribers, not equity owners.

**Executive Management**: The Chief Executive Officer (CEO) and other C-level executives responsible for day-to-day operations of the Company.

**Independent Director**: A Board Member who has no material relationship with the Company other than their directorship, ensuring objective oversight.

**Executive Director**: A Board Member who also holds an executive management position within the Company (e.g., CEO, CFO).

**Ordinary Resolution**: A decision requiring a simple majority (more than 50%) of votes cast.

**Special Resolution**: A decision requiring at least 75% of votes cast, typically for major corporate actions.


**Quorum**: The minimum number of Board Members or Shareholders required to be present for a meeting to conduct official business.

**Conflict of Interest**: A situation where a Board Member or Shareholder has a personal or financial interest that could influence their judgment or decisions.

**Venture Builder Project**: A co-investment opportunity where MyGrowNet members can become shareholders in newly formed limited companies (separate from MyGrowNet).

**Profit-Sharing**: Distribution of a portion of Company profits to platform members based on their activity and engagement (not equity ownership).

**Dividend**: Distribution of Company profits to shareholders based on their equity ownership.

**Material Interest**: A significant financial or personal stake in a matter that could affect decision-making objectivity.

**Related Party**: Any person or entity with a close relationship to a Board Member or Shareholder that could create conflicts of interest.

## 3. Board of Directors

### 3.1 Composition of the Board

**Board Size:**
- Minimum: 3 Directors
- Maximum: 9 Directors
- Optimal: 5-7 Directors for effective governance

**Board Composition:**
- At least one (1) Executive Director (typically the CEO)
- At least two (2) Independent Directors
- Additional Non-Executive Directors as needed
- At least one (1) Director with financial expertise

**Diversity Considerations:**
- The Board should strive for diversity in skills, experience, gender, and background
- Representation should reflect the Company's stakeholder base
- Expertise should cover: finance, technology, legal, marketing, operations

### 3.2 Appointment of Board Members

**Nomination Process:**
1. Shareholders may nominate candidates for Board positions
2. Nominations must be submitted in writing at least 30 days before the Annual General Meeting (AGM)
3. Nominees must provide:
   - Curriculum vitae and professional background
   - Declaration of any conflicts of interest
   - Consent to serve if elected
   - References from professional contacts

**Eligibility Requirements:**
- Must be at least 21 years of age
- Must not be disqualified under the Companies Act
- Must not be an undischarged bankrupt
- Must not have been convicted of fraud or dishonesty
- Must possess relevant skills, experience, or expertise
- Must be able to dedicate sufficient time to Board duties

**Appointment Method:**
- Directors are elected by Ordinary Resolution at the AGM
- Shareholders vote based on their shareholding percentage
- Appointed Directors serve from the date of appointment

**Initial Board:**
- Founding Directors may be appointed in the Articles of Association
- Serve until the first AGM, then subject to election

### 3.3 Term of Office

**Standard Term:**
- Directors serve for a term of three (3) years
- Terms are staggered to ensure continuity
- Directors are eligible for re-election

**Term Limits:**
- No Director shall serve more than three (3) consecutive terms (9 years) without a break
- After 9 consecutive years, a Director must take at least one (1) year break before re-election
- Exception: Founding Directors may serve longer with Shareholder approval

**Retirement Rotation:**
- One-third of Directors retire by rotation each year
- Retiring Directors are eligible for re-election
- Ensures regular Board renewal and fresh perspectives


### 3.4 Removal of Board Members

**Grounds for Removal:**
- Failure to attend three (3) consecutive Board meetings without valid reason
- Breach of fiduciary duties or Code of Conduct
- Conviction of a criminal offense involving dishonesty
- Mental or physical incapacity to perform duties
- Bankruptcy or insolvency
- Material conflict of interest that cannot be resolved
- Loss of confidence by Shareholders

**Removal Process:**
1. Notice of proposed removal must be given to the Director and all Shareholders at least 21 days before the meeting
2. The Director has the right to be heard at the meeting
3. Removal requires a Special Resolution (75% of votes)
4. The Director ceases to hold office immediately upon passing of the resolution

**Voluntary Resignation:**
- A Director may resign by giving written notice to the Board
- Notice period: At least 30 days (or as agreed)
- Resignation takes effect on the date specified or upon acceptance by the Board

### 3.5 Board Roles and Responsibilities

**Primary Responsibilities:**

1. **Strategic Oversight**
   - Approve and monitor the Company's strategic plan
   - Set long-term objectives and direction
   - Review and approve annual budgets and business plans
   - Monitor performance against strategic goals

2. **Governance and Compliance**
   - Ensure compliance with all applicable laws and regulations
   - Approve and monitor corporate policies
   - Oversee risk management framework
   - Ensure proper financial controls and reporting

3. **Financial Stewardship**
   - Review and approve financial statements
   - Approve major capital expenditures and investments
   - Declare dividends (subject to Shareholder approval)
   - Ensure financial sustainability and solvency

4. **Executive Oversight**
   - Appoint and evaluate the CEO
   - Approve appointments of C-level executives
   - Set executive compensation and incentives
   - Succession planning for key positions

5. **Stakeholder Relations**
   - Protect interests of Shareholders
   - Ensure fair treatment of platform members
   - Maintain relationships with regulators
   - Oversee corporate communications

6. **Risk Management**
   - Identify and assess major risks
   - Approve risk management policies
   - Monitor risk mitigation strategies
   - Ensure business continuity planning

**Specific Duties:**
- Attend all Board meetings (minimum 75% attendance required)
- Prepare for meetings by reviewing materials in advance
- Act in good faith and in the best interests of the Company
- Exercise independent judgment
- Maintain confidentiality of Board discussions and Company information
- Disclose conflicts of interest promptly
- Participate actively in Board committees as assigned

**Limitations:**
- The Board does NOT manage day-to-day operations (delegated to Executive Management)
- The Board does NOT interfere with member operations, commissions, or platform activities
- The Board does NOT make operational decisions without Executive Management input
- The Board acts collectively, not as individual Directors

### 3.6 Board Meetings

**Regular Meetings:**
- The Board shall meet at least quarterly (four times per year)
- Additional meetings may be called as needed
- Meeting schedule set annually in advance

**Notice Requirements:**
- At least 14 days' written notice for regular meetings
- At least 7 days' notice for special meetings
- Notice may be waived by unanimous consent

**Quorum:**
- Quorum is a majority of Directors (more than 50%)
- No business may be conducted without quorum
- If quorum is lost during a meeting, the meeting is adjourned

**Voting:**
- Each Director has one vote
- Decisions made by simple majority
- Chairperson has a casting vote in case of tie
- Dissenting Directors may request their dissent be recorded in minutes

**Minutes:**
- Detailed minutes of all meetings must be kept
- Minutes approved at the next meeting
- Minutes are confidential but available to all Directors and Shareholders

**Attendance:**
- Directors may attend in person or via video/teleconference
- Proxy voting is not permitted for Directors
- Consistent non-attendance may result in removal


### 3.7 Board Committees

The Board may establish committees to assist in its duties:

**1. Audit and Risk Committee**

**Composition:**
- Minimum 3 Directors
- Majority must be Independent Directors
- At least one member with financial expertise
- Chairperson must be an Independent Director

**Responsibilities:**
- Review financial statements and reports
- Oversee internal and external audits
- Monitor internal controls and risk management
- Review compliance with financial regulations
- Assess fraud prevention measures

**Meetings:** At least quarterly

---

**2. Compensation and Nomination Committee**

**Composition:**
- Minimum 3 Directors
- Majority must be Independent Directors
- CEO may attend but not vote

**Responsibilities:**
- Review and recommend executive compensation
- Evaluate CEO and executive performance
- Nominate candidates for Board positions
- Plan for executive succession
- Review Board composition and effectiveness

**Meetings:** At least twice annually

---

**3. Technology and Innovation Committee**

**Composition:**
- Minimum 3 Directors
- At least one member with technology expertise
- CTO may attend as advisor

**Responsibilities:**
- Review technology strategy and roadmap
- Assess cybersecurity and data protection
- Evaluate technology investments
- Monitor platform performance and scalability
- Oversee innovation initiatives

**Meetings:** At least quarterly

---

**4. Venture Investment Committee**

**Composition:**
- Minimum 3 Directors
- At least one member with investment experience
- Chief Venture Officer may attend as advisor

**Responsibilities:**
- Review and approve Venture Builder projects
- Assess investment opportunities
- Monitor portfolio performance
- Ensure due diligence standards
- Oversee member co-investment activities

**Meetings:** Monthly or as needed

---

**Committee Authority:**
- Committees make recommendations to the full Board
- Only the full Board can make final decisions on major matters
- Committees may be delegated specific decision-making authority within defined limits
- Committee minutes are reported to the full Board

## 4. Shareholders

### 4.1 Classes of Shares

**Ordinary Shares:**
- Standard equity ownership in the Company
- One vote per share
- Entitled to dividends when declared
- Entitled to distribution of assets upon liquidation

**Preference Shares (if issued):**
- Priority in dividend payments
- Priority in asset distribution upon liquidation
- May have limited or no voting rights
- Terms specified in share certificate

**Founder Shares (if applicable):**
- Issued to founding shareholders
- May have enhanced voting rights (e.g., 10 votes per share)
- Subject to vesting schedules
- May have transfer restrictions

### 4.2 Shareholder Rights

**Voting Rights:**
- Vote at General Meetings on all resolutions
- One vote per ordinary share held
- Vote on election and removal of Directors
- Vote on major corporate actions (mergers, acquisitions, amendments)
- Vote on dividend declarations

**Information Rights:**
- Receive annual financial statements
- Receive notice of all General Meetings
- Access to Company records (subject to confidentiality)
- Receive reports on Company performance
- Inspect minutes of General Meetings

**Financial Rights:**
- Receive dividends when declared and paid
- Share in distribution of assets upon liquidation
- Pre-emptive rights on new share issuances (if provided in Articles)
- Right to transfer shares (subject to restrictions)

**Governance Rights:**
- Nominate candidates for Board positions
- Propose resolutions at General Meetings
- Requisition Special General Meetings (with sufficient shareholding)
- Challenge Board decisions through proper channels
- Bring derivative actions on behalf of the Company (in extreme cases)


### 4.3 Shareholder Responsibilities

**Financial Obligations:**
- Pay for shares in full when called
- Meet any additional capital calls (if provided in Articles)
- Maintain accurate contact information with the Company

**Governance Participation:**
- Attend General Meetings or appoint proxies
- Vote responsibly on resolutions
- Stay informed about Company performance
- Support the Company's strategic direction

**Compliance:**
- Comply with the Companies Act and Company policies
- Respect confidentiality of non-public information
- Disclose conflicts of interest when relevant
- Act in good faith toward the Company

**Limitations:**
- Shareholders do NOT manage the Company (delegated to Board and Management)
- Shareholders do NOT have authority over operational decisions
- Shareholders do NOT have access to member data or operational systems
- Shareholders act through proper governance channels only

### 4.4 Transfer of Shares

**Transfer Restrictions:**
- Shares may be subject to transfer restrictions in the Articles of Association
- Right of first refusal may apply (existing Shareholders have priority)
- Board approval may be required for transfers
- Transfers must comply with securities regulations

**Transfer Process:**
1. Shareholder notifies the Company of intention to transfer
2. Company notifies other Shareholders of the offer (if right of first refusal applies)
3. Existing Shareholders have 30 days to exercise right of first refusal
4. If not exercised, transfer may proceed to third party (subject to Board approval)
5. Transfer instrument executed and registered with the Company
6. Share certificate issued to new Shareholder

**Prohibited Transfers:**
- Transfers that would violate securities laws
- Transfers to disqualified persons
- Transfers that would result in the Company becoming a public company
- Transfers without proper documentation

### 4.5 General Meetings

**Annual General Meeting (AGM):**
- Must be held within 6 months of financial year-end
- All Shareholders entitled to attend
- Business includes:
  - Presentation of financial statements
  - Election of Directors
  - Appointment of auditors
  - Declaration of dividends
  - Any other business

**Special General Meetings (SGM):**
- May be called by the Board or requisitioned by Shareholders holding at least 10% of shares
- Called for specific urgent matters
- Requires at least 21 days' notice

**Notice Requirements:**
- At least 21 days' written notice for AGM
- At least 14 days' notice for SGM
- Notice must include agenda, date, time, location
- Financial statements must accompany AGM notice

**Quorum:**
- Quorum is Shareholders holding at least 50% of issued shares
- If quorum not present within 30 minutes, meeting adjourned
- Adjourned meeting held within 7 days with same quorum requirement

**Voting:**
- Voting by show of hands (one vote per Shareholder) or poll (one vote per share)
- Any Shareholder may demand a poll
- Proxy voting permitted with written authorization
- Electronic voting may be permitted

**Resolutions:**
- Ordinary Resolution: Requires more than 50% of votes cast
- Special Resolution: Requires at least 75% of votes cast
- Written resolutions permitted if signed by all Shareholders

## 5. Participation of Board Members and Shareholders as Platform Members

### 5.1 Eligibility to Participate

**General Principle:**
Board Members and Shareholders are permitted to participate as MyGrowNet platform members, subject to the restrictions and guidelines outlined in this policy.

**Permitted Activities:**
- Subscribe to MyGrowNet products and services
- Participate in training programs and workshops
- Purchase from MyGrow Shop
- Use the MyGrow Save wallet
- Participate in Venture Builder co-investments (as separate shareholders in new companies)

**Restricted Activities:**
- Board Members and Shareholders may NOT participate in the referral/MLM network in a manner that creates unfair advantage
- May NOT use insider information for personal gain in member activities
- May NOT receive preferential treatment in commission calculations
- May NOT manipulate platform algorithms or systems


### 5.2 Referral Network Participation

**Executive Directors:**
- The CEO and other Executive Directors are **PROHIBITED** from participating in the referral/MLM network
- This prevents conflicts of interest and ensures operational integrity
- Executive Directors may use products/services for personal use only

**Non-Executive Directors:**
- Non-Executive Directors may participate in the referral network with the following conditions:
  1. Must disclose participation to the full Board
  2. Must recuse themselves from decisions affecting member commissions or network structure
  3. Must not use Board position to gain unfair advantage
  4. Subject to annual review by the Board

**Shareholders (Non-Directors):**
- Shareholders who are not Directors may participate freely in the referral network
- Must comply with all member terms and conditions
- Must not use Shareholder status to gain unfair advantage
- Must not claim special privileges or preferential treatment

**Immediate Family Members:**
- Immediate family members of Board Members and Shareholders may participate in the referral network
- Family members must be disclosed to the Board
- Same restrictions apply to prevent conflicts of interest

### 5.3 Venture Builder Participation

**Board Members and Shareholders:**
- May participate in Venture Builder co-investment opportunities
- Must disclose participation in specific projects to the Board
- Must recuse themselves from Board decisions on projects they invest in
- Subject to same terms and conditions as other member investors

**Investment Limits:**
- Board Members and Shareholders may not invest more than 25% of any single Venture Builder project
- This prevents undue influence over project governance
- Ensures broad participation by regular members

**Governance Separation:**
- Venture Builder projects form separate legal entities
- Board Members/Shareholders of MyGrowNet do not automatically become directors of Venture Builder companies
- Each Venture Builder company has its own governance structure

### 5.4 Profit-Sharing and Commissions

**Profit-Sharing Distribution:**
- Board Members and Shareholders who are also platform members receive profit-sharing based on their Bonus Points (BP), same as all members
- No preferential treatment or enhanced distribution
- Calculated using the same formula: (Individual BP / Total BP) × 60% of monthly profit

**Commission Calculations:**
- Board Members and Shareholders who participate in the referral network receive commissions based on the standard commission structure
- No enhanced rates or special bonuses
- Subject to same qualification requirements (Monthly Activity Points)

**Transparency:**
- All commission and profit-sharing payments to Board Members and Shareholders must be disclosed in financial reports
- Annual disclosure of total payments to related parties
- Subject to audit review

### 5.5 Restrictions and Safeguards

**Prohibited Actions:**
1. Using Board or Shareholder position to recruit members
2. Claiming endorsement or special status in marketing materials
3. Accessing member data for personal network building
4. Manipulating commission calculations or network placements
5. Receiving advance information on platform changes for personal advantage
6. Pressuring employees or contractors to join their network

**Monitoring and Enforcement:**
- Compliance Officer monitors participation of Board Members and Shareholders
- Quarterly reports to the Audit and Risk Committee
- Violations may result in:
  - Suspension of member account
  - Removal from Board
  - Forfeiture of commissions
  - Legal action if fraud is involved

**Annual Declaration:**
- All Board Members and Shareholders must annually declare:
  - Whether they participate as platform members
  - Their member ID and network position
  - Any family members who are platform members
  - Any Venture Builder investments

## 6. Conflict of Interest Policy

### 6.1 Definition of Conflict of Interest

A conflict of interest arises when a Board Member or Shareholder has a personal, financial, or professional interest that could:
- Influence their judgment or decisions
- Compromise their objectivity
- Create an appearance of impropriety
- Compete with the Company's interests

**Types of Conflicts:**

**Direct Financial Interest:**
- Ownership in competing businesses
- Contracts or transactions with the Company
- Personal loans or guarantees involving the Company

**Indirect Financial Interest:**
- Family members' business interests
- Investments in suppliers or partners
- Employment or consulting relationships

**Personal Relationships:**
- Family members employed by the Company
- Close personal relationships with vendors or partners
- Romantic relationships within the organization

**Professional Conflicts:**
- Serving on boards of competing companies
- Consulting for industry competitors
- Holding positions that create divided loyalties


### 6.2 Disclosure Requirements

**Timing of Disclosure:**
- Upon appointment to the Board
- Annually through a written declaration
- Immediately when a new conflict arises
- Before any Board discussion on a matter where conflict exists

**Method of Disclosure:**
- Written disclosure to the Board Chairperson
- Recorded in the Register of Interests
- Noted in Board meeting minutes when relevant
- Disclosed in annual financial statements (for material interests)

**Information to Disclose:**
- Nature of the interest or relationship
- Parties involved
- Financial value (if applicable)
- Duration of the interest
- Any actions taken to mitigate the conflict

### 6.3 Management of Conflicts

**Recusal Process:**
1. Board Member declares the conflict at the start of discussion
2. Board Member leaves the meeting during discussion and voting
3. Board Member does not receive materials related to the conflicted matter
4. Decision is made by remaining Board Members
5. Recusal is recorded in meeting minutes

**Abstention from Voting:**
- Board Members with conflicts must abstain from voting
- Abstention is recorded in minutes
- Quorum is calculated excluding the conflicted Director

**Independent Review:**
- Material conflicts may require independent legal or financial review
- Independent Directors assess whether the conflict is manageable
- Board may require divestment or resignation if conflict is severe

**Ongoing Monitoring:**
- Compliance Officer maintains Register of Interests
- Annual review of all declared interests
- Quarterly reporting to Audit and Risk Committee

### 6.4 Related Party Transactions

**Definition:**
Transactions between the Company and:
- Board Members or Shareholders
- Executive Management
- Immediate family members of the above
- Entities controlled by the above

**Approval Process:**
1. Full disclosure of the transaction and relationship
2. Independent valuation or market comparison
3. Approval by disinterested Board Members
4. Documentation of the business rationale
5. Ongoing monitoring of the transaction

**Prohibited Transactions:**
- Loans to Board Members or Shareholders (except in ordinary course of business)
- Transactions on terms more favorable than market rates
- Transactions that benefit individuals at the Company's expense
- Transactions that create unmanageable conflicts

**Disclosure Requirements:**
- All related party transactions disclosed in financial statements
- Annual summary provided to Shareholders
- Material transactions require Shareholder approval

### 6.5 Penalties for Non-Disclosure

**Consequences:**
- Removal from Board
- Forfeiture of compensation related to the conflicted matter
- Reversal of the transaction
- Legal action for breach of fiduciary duty
- Reporting to regulatory authorities if fraud is involved

## 7. Code of Conduct and Ethics

### 7.1 Fiduciary Duties

**Duty of Care:**
- Act with the care, diligence, and skill of a reasonably prudent person
- Stay informed about the Company's business and affairs
- Attend meetings and prepare adequately
- Ask questions and seek clarification when needed
- Make informed decisions based on available information

**Duty of Loyalty:**
- Act in good faith and in the best interests of the Company
- Place Company interests above personal interests
- Avoid conflicts of interest
- Not compete with the Company
- Not usurp corporate opportunities for personal gain

**Duty of Obedience:**
- Comply with applicable laws and regulations
- Adhere to the Company's Articles of Association
- Follow Board policies and procedures
- Ensure the Company operates within its legal mandate

### 7.2 Ethical Standards

**Integrity:**
- Conduct business with honesty and transparency
- Maintain high ethical standards in all dealings
- Avoid deceptive or misleading practices
- Report unethical behavior promptly

**Respect:**
- Treat all stakeholders with dignity and respect
- Value diversity and inclusion
- Foster a culture of mutual respect
- Prohibit discrimination and harassment

**Accountability:**
- Take responsibility for decisions and actions
- Accept consequences of mistakes
- Learn from failures and improve
- Hold others accountable to the same standards

**Transparency:**
- Communicate openly and honestly
- Provide accurate and timely information
- Disclose material information to stakeholders
- Avoid secrecy except where legally required


### 7.3 Specific Conduct Guidelines

**Confidentiality:**
- Maintain confidentiality of Board discussions and Company information
- Do not disclose non-public information to third parties
- Do not use confidential information for personal gain
- Protect member data and privacy
- Confidentiality obligations continue after leaving the Board

**Insider Trading:**
- Do not trade Company shares based on non-public information
- Do not tip others with insider information
- Comply with securities laws and regulations
- Observe blackout periods before financial announcements

**Corporate Opportunities:**
- Do not divert business opportunities that belong to the Company
- Disclose opportunities that may be of interest to the Company
- Obtain Board approval before pursuing opportunities related to Company business

**Use of Company Assets:**
- Use Company assets only for legitimate business purposes
- Do not use Company resources for personal benefit
- Protect Company intellectual property
- Return all Company property upon leaving the Board

**External Communications:**
- Only authorized spokespersons may speak on behalf of the Company
- Board Members must not make unauthorized public statements
- Refer media inquiries to the CEO or designated spokesperson
- Social media posts must not disclose confidential information

### 7.4 Compliance and Reporting

**Compliance Obligations:**
- Comply with all applicable laws and regulations
- Complete required training and certifications
- Cooperate with audits and investigations
- Report violations of law or policy

**Whistleblower Protection:**
- The Company protects individuals who report violations in good faith
- No retaliation against whistleblowers
- Anonymous reporting channels available
- Investigations conducted confidentially

**Reporting Violations:**
- Report suspected violations to:
  - Board Chairperson
  - Compliance Officer
  - Audit and Risk Committee
  - Anonymous hotline (if established)
- Reports investigated promptly and thoroughly
- Appropriate action taken based on findings

## 9. Profit Distribution Framework

### 9.1 Company Profit Allocation

**Profit Distribution Model:**

When the Company generates profits, they are allocated as follows:

1. **Operating Reserves (20%)**
   - Retained for working capital
   - Emergency fund
   - Business continuity

2. **Growth and Investment (20%)**
   - Technology upgrades
   - Market expansion
   - New product development
   - Infrastructure improvements

3. **Member Profit-Sharing Pool (40%)**
   - Distributed to active platform members
   - Based on Bonus Points (BP) system
   - Monthly distribution
   - Incentivizes member engagement

4. **Shareholder Dividends (20%)**
   - Distributed to equity Shareholders
   - Based on shareholding percentage
   - Quarterly or annual distribution
   - Subject to Board declaration

**Important Distinction:**
- **Member Profit-Sharing** = Operational expense, paid to members for platform activity (like commissions)
- **Shareholder Dividends** = Distribution of net profits to equity owners


### 9.2 Member Profit-Sharing (Not Shareholder Dividends)

**Nature of Member Profit-Sharing:**
- Members are NOT equity owners
- Profit-sharing is a **performance-based incentive**, not a return on investment
- Members earn based on activity, not capital contribution
- Treated as operational expense, not profit distribution

**Calculation Method:**
```
Member Share = (Individual BP / Total BP) × Member Profit-Sharing Pool
```

**Eligibility:**
- Active members who meet Monthly Activity Points (MAP) requirements
- Based on platform engagement and contribution
- No minimum investment required
- Transparent and auditable

**Legal Compliance:**
- Not a deposit-taking activity
- Not an investment scheme
- Complies with Bank of Zambia regulations
- Members are customers/subscribers, not investors

### 9.3 Shareholder Dividend Policy

**Dividend Declaration:**
- Board recommends dividend amount
- Shareholders approve at General Meeting
- Payment made within 30 days of approval

**Dividend Criteria:**
- Company must be profitable
- Company must pass solvency test
- Adequate reserves must be maintained
- Cash flow must support payment

**Dividend Frequency:**
- Interim dividends: May be declared quarterly
- Final dividend: Declared at AGM based on annual results
- Special dividends: May be declared for exceptional profits

**Dividend Payment:**
- Paid in cash to registered Shareholders
- Proportional to shareholding
- Subject to withholding tax (if applicable)
- Payment via bank transfer or mobile money

**Retained Earnings:**
- Board may retain earnings for growth and investment
- Retained earnings increase Company value
- Benefits Shareholders through capital appreciation

### 9.4 Transparency and Reporting

- Disclosure of related party transactions

**Member Communication:**

- Transparent calculation methodology
- Clear distinction from Shareholder dividends
- Educational materials on the difference

**Regulatory Compliance:**
- Annual filing with PACRA
- Compliance with Bank of Zambia regulations
- Tax compliance (ZRA)
- Securities regulations (if applicable)

## 10. Decision-Making Procedures

### 10.1 Board Decision-Making

**Matters Requiring Board Approval:**

**Strategic Decisions:**
- Approval of strategic plan and annual budget
- Major changes to business model
- Entry into new markets or business lines
- Mergers, acquisitions, or disposals
- Issuance of new shares

**Financial Decisions:**
- Capital expenditures above K500,000
- Borrowing above K1,000,000
- Investment decisions above K500,000
- Declaration of dividends
- Write-offs above K100,000

**Governance Decisions:**
- Appointment and removal of CEO
- Appointment of C-level executives
- Approval of major policies
- Changes to organizational structure
- Related party transactions above K50,000

**Operational Oversight:**
- Review of quarterly financial results
- Approval of annual financial statements
- Risk management framework
- Compliance reports
- Major contracts above K500,000

**Decision Process:**
1. Management prepares proposal with supporting documents
2. Proposal circulated to Board at least 7 days before meeting
3. Board discusses and asks questions
4. Board votes on the proposal
5. Decision recorded in minutes
6. Management implements approved decisions

**Delegation of Authority:**
- Board may delegate certain decisions to committees or management
- Delegation must be documented in writing
- Limits and conditions clearly specified
- Regular reporting on delegated decisions

### 10.2 Shareholder Decision-Making

**Matters Requiring Ordinary Resolution (>50%):**
- Election of Directors
- Appointment of auditors
- Approval of annual financial statements
- Approval of final dividend
- Routine business matters

**Matters Requiring Special Resolution (≥75%):**
- Amendment of Articles of Association
- Change of Company name
- Increase or reduction of share capital
- Removal of Directors
- Voluntary liquidation
- Major asset disposals
- Mergers or acquisitions

**Shareholder Voting Process:**
1. Notice of General Meeting sent to all Shareholders
2. Agenda and supporting documents provided
3. Shareholders attend in person or appoint proxies
4. Resolutions presented and discussed
5. Vote taken (show of hands or poll)
6. Results announced and recorded

**Proxy Voting:**
- Shareholders may appoint proxies to vote on their behalf
- Proxy form must be submitted at least 48 hours before meeting
- Proxy may be another Shareholder or any other person
- Proxy votes according to Shareholder's instructions


### 10.3 Emergency Decision-Making

**Emergency Situations:**
- Situations requiring immediate action to protect the Company
- Natural disasters, cyberattacks, regulatory actions
- Sudden market changes or competitive threats
- Legal emergencies

**Emergency Procedures:**
1. CEO assesses the situation and determines if emergency powers are needed
2. CEO contacts Board Chairperson immediately
3. Chairperson may authorize emergency action or call emergency Board meeting
4. Emergency decisions ratified at next regular Board meeting
5. Full documentation of emergency actions provided to Board

**Limitations:**
- Emergency powers limited to immediate protective actions
- Cannot be used for strategic decisions or major transactions
- Must be reported to full Board within 48 hours
- Subject to Board review and ratification

### 11.1 Confidential Information

**Definition:**
Confidential information includes:
- Financial data and projections
- Strategic plans and business models
- Member data and analytics
- Technology and intellectual property
- Board discussions and decisions
- Pending transactions or negotiations
- Legal matters and disputes

**Obligations:**
- Board Members and Shareholders must maintain strict confidentiality
- Information may not be disclosed to third parties
- Information may not be used for personal gain
- Confidentiality continues after leaving the Board or selling shares

**Exceptions:**
- Information already in the public domain
- Information required to be disclosed by law
- Information disclosed with Board authorization
- Information disclosed to professional advisors under confidentiality agreements

### 11.2 Member Data Protection

**Data Privacy Principles:**
- Board Members and Shareholders do not have access to individual member data
- Member data used only for legitimate business purposes
- Compliance with data protection laws
- Strong security measures to protect member information

**Prohibited Uses:**
- Using member data for personal business purposes
- Sharing member data with third parties without consent
- Using member data for marketing outside the platform
- Accessing member accounts without authorization

**Reporting:**
- Board receives aggregated, anonymized data only
- Individual member information protected
- Data breaches reported immediately
- Compliance with data protection regulations

### 11.3 Intellectual Property

**Company IP:**
- All intellectual property developed for the Company belongs to the Company
- Board Members and Shareholders have no personal rights to Company IP
- IP includes: trademarks, software, business processes, content

**Protection:**
- Board ensures proper IP registration and protection
- Confidentiality agreements with employees and contractors
- Non-disclosure agreements with partners
- Legal action against IP infringement

## 13. Indemnification and Insurance

### 13.1 Indemnification of Directors

**Scope of Indemnification:**
The Company shall indemnify Directors against:
- Legal costs and expenses incurred in defending claims
- Judgments and settlements (except in cases of willful misconduct or fraud)
- Regulatory fines (except for personal violations)

**Conditions:**
- Director acted in good faith
- Director acted in the best interests of the Company
- Director's conduct was not fraudulent or criminal
- Director cooperated with the Company's defense

**Limitations:**
- No indemnification for willful misconduct or fraud
- No indemnification for personal profit at Company's expense
- No indemnification for violations of fiduciary duties
- Subject to legal limits under Zambian law

### 13.2 Directors and Officers Insurance

**Coverage:**
- The Company shall maintain Directors and Officers (D&O) insurance
- Coverage for legal defense costs and liability
- Protects personal assets of Directors
- Coverage limits appropriate to Company size and risk

**Policy Terms:**
- Reviewed annually by Audit and Risk Committee
- Coverage limits adjusted as Company grows
- All Directors and officers covered
- Policy terms disclosed to Board

### 13.3 Limitation of Liability

**Legal Protections:**
- Directors not personally liable for Company debts (except in cases of fraud)
- Limited liability for decisions made in good faith
- Business judgment rule applies to strategic decisions
- Protection under Companies Act provisions

**Exceptions:**
- Personal liability for fraud or criminal conduct
- Liability for breach of fiduciary duties
- Liability for unauthorized personal benefits
- Liability for violations of law

## 17. Dispute Resolution

### 17.1 Internal Disputes

**Board Disputes:**
- Disputes between Board Members resolved through discussion
- Chairperson mediates disputes
- If unresolved, matter referred to full Board
- Independent advisor may be engaged if needed

**Shareholder Disputes:**
- Disputes between Shareholders resolved through negotiation
- Mediation by independent third party if needed
- Arbitration as alternative to litigation
- Legal action as last resort

**Board-Shareholder Disputes:**
- Open communication and transparency
- Special meetings to address concerns
- Independent review if needed
- Compliance with Companies Act procedures

### 17.2 Dispute Resolution Process

**Step 1: Direct Discussion**
- Parties attempt to resolve dispute directly
- Good faith negotiations
- Documentation of discussions

**Step 2: Mediation**
- If direct discussion fails, engage neutral mediator
- Mediation conducted confidentially
- Non-binding recommendations

**Step 3: Arbitration**
- If mediation fails, proceed to arbitration
- Arbitrator appointed by mutual agreement or by arbitration body
- Binding decision
- Faster and less expensive than litigation

**Step 4: Litigation**
- Last resort if all other methods fail
- Zambian courts have jurisdiction
- Governed by Zambian law
- Legal costs may be awarded to prevailing party

### 17.3 Governing Law and Jurisdiction

**Applicable Law:**
- This policy governed by the laws of Zambia
- Interpretation according to Zambian legal principles
- Compliance with Zambian regulations

**Jurisdiction:**
- Zambian courts have exclusive jurisdiction
- Venue: Lusaka, Zambia (or as specified in Articles)
- Parties submit to jurisdiction of Zambian courts

## 20. Conclusion

This Board Members and Shareholders Policy establishes a comprehensive governance framework for MyGrowNet that:

✅ **Defines clear roles and responsibilities** for Board Members and Shareholders  
✅ **Ensures legal compliance** with Zambian law and regulations  
✅ **Protects all stakeholders** including Board Members, Shareholders, and platform members  
✅ **Maintains separation** between corporate governance and member operations  
✅ **Prevents conflicts of interest** through disclosure and management procedures  
✅ **Promotes transparency and accountability** in all governance matters

1. **Separation of Roles**: Clear distinction between Shareholders (equity owners), Board Members (governance), and Members (platform subscribers)

3. **Transparency**: Open communication and disclosure of all material matters

4. **Compliance**: Adherence to all applicable laws, regulations, and ethical standards

5. **Fairness**: Equal treatment of all stakeholders within their respective roles

6. **Accountability**: Clear lines of responsibility and performance evaluation

- Distributed to all Board Members and Shareholders
